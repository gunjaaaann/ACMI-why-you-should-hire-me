G's lens

Hi, I'm Gunjan!

Ever since I can remember, I’ve been turning the world’s leftovers into my creative playground. Transforming trash into treasure—literally. As a kid I would collect little things that people might overlook—scraps of paper, broken buttons, old CDs, even stray paper cups from restaurants and would turn these tiny fragments into cards for my mum, collages, or whatever creation my imagination demanded. My parents would laugh and say I’d never let anything go to waste, but really, it was my first lesson in seeing potential where others didn’t—and in making something out of nothing.

In high school I taught myself how to use the Adobe Creative Suite. And that was it. That curiosity pushed me to pack my bags and move across the globe to pursue my dream and study digital desgin at UniMelb. These days, I often describe myself as “diagnosed with a constant need for a side gig.” If I’m not deep in student media at uni, you’ll probably find me volunteering and designing for conferences (see you at UX Camp next week?). If not that, I’m either sweating it out at the gym or being a full-on foodie, hunting down new spots around the city.

I’m not sharing all this for no reason — I want you to get a sense of who I am and how passionate I am about this role. So, here are five reasons I think you should hire me:

Because I see code as a canvas

I’m as comfortable in Figma as I am in VS Code — designing experiences that don’t just work, but feel right. I bring creativity to logic and logic to creativity.

Because I ask a lot of questions

Every great design starts with curiosity. I poke, prod, and wander until I understand audiences and what makes them tick.

Because design is only good if everyone can use it

I care deeply about inclusive design - creating experiences that welcome everyone in. My best work happens when everyone is invited to the experience.

Because I don't believe in a solo genius

Collaboration keeps me curious. I learn the most when I’m surrounded by people who think differently from me.

Because I like making things that matter

I make things with my heart, projects that mean something - often about people and the planet. My favourite projects often have something to say.

Thanks for reading my story and considering my application!
